Wedge Pattern in Trend

Wedge Pattern

The wedge trading strategy is a reversal trading strategy that has the potential to generate big profits. It is a pure price action trading strategy. A wedge pattern is a type of chart pattern that is formed by converging two trend lines. Wedges are the type of continuation as well as the reversal chart patterns.

There are two types of Wedge Pattern


1. Falling Wedge Pattern:
Falling Wedge

Falling wedge in a downtrend is a decline to a new low on strong volume, several weeks of range-bound trade characterized by lower lows and lower highs with contracting volume, followed by a sharp break higher on strong volume.

How to Trade Falling Wedge:
  • Identify the bearish trend before the pattern develops. This is because it’s a reversal pattern.
  • Identify pattern which looks like a triangle that is pointing down.
  • Draw a trend line on a series of lower highs followed by a series of lower lows. This froms a Support and Resistance
  • Wait for the Resistance Breakout
  • If the close price crosses the Resistance line with a big candlesticks, This confirm the breakout
  • Increase in volume near the Resistance line determines the perfect breakout
  • The falling wedge pattern is not confirmed until it’s breaking to the upside resistance.
Falling Wedge

2. Rising Wedge Pattern:
Rising Wedge

A rising wedge is a bearish pattern. It makes higher lows faster than it makes higher highs.

Rising Wedge in an uptrend is a rally to a new high on strong volume, several weeks of narrowing, range-bound trade characterized by higher highs and higher lows with contracting volume,followed by a sharp break lower on strong volume

How to Trade Rising Wedge:
  • this is exactly opposite to the Falling Wedge Pattern
  • Identify the up trend before the pattern develops.
  • Identify pattern which looks like a triangle that is pointing up.
  • Draw a trend line on a series of Higher lows followed by a series of Higher high. This froms a Support and Resistance
  • Wait for the Support Breakout
  • If the close price crosses the Support line with a big candlesticks, This confirm the breakout
  • Increase in volume near the Support line determines the perfect breakout
  • The Rising wedge pattern is not confirmed until it’s breaking to the downside Support.
Rising Wedge
Timeframe:
  • Applys to all timeframe
Target TAKE PROFIT:
  • Measure the size of the pattern(height) from the initial points of Support and Resistance and then apply the same distance after the breakout point. This would be your minimum target profit.
  • Most of the times target will be higher than the aobve mentioned step.
Stop LOSS:
  • Falling Wedges: After breaking resistance, Falling Wedge's Support will be the Stop Loss
  • Rising Wedges: After breaking of support, Rising Wedge's Resistance will be the Stop Loss
False Breakout hits Stop Loss:
  • Most of the time, the market or stock might go apposite to the pattern. To avoid big loss, apply Stop Loss.
