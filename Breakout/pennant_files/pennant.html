Pennant patterns

The behavior of Pennant patterns are exactly similar to the Wedge Patterns. The main difference between Pennants and Wedge patterns is pennants patterns structure are Sideways and Horizontal. Whereas the wedges are either ascending or descending.

Pennant V/s Wedges

Pennant is a reversal trading strategy. The pattern looks like a small symmetrical triangle. These are the type of continuation as well as the reversal chart patterns.

There are two types of Pennant Pattern. Depending on the direction of the movement, Pennant patterns are usually described as being bearish or bullish.


1. Bullish Pennant:
Bullish Pennant

Bullish pennants can form after an uptrend. The pattern is completed when price breaks out of the triangle in the direction of the preceding trend, at which point it will likely continue in this direction

2. Bearish Pennant:
Bearish Pennant

Bearish Pennants are simply the opposite of the Bullish Pennant. Bearish Pennants are continuation patterns that occur in strong downtrends.


Pennant pattern Rules:
  • A Pennant pattern has to be preceded by a strong up or down move that resembles a flagpole. If there isnâ€™t a flagpole, then it's a triangle and not a Pennant.
  • A Pennant tends to form a shallow retracement (typically less than 38% of the flagpole). A deep retracement is indicative of a triangle rather than a Pennant.
  • A Pennant is characterized by the continuation of the upward or downward trend.
  • A Pennant is a short-term pattern that is usually completed within 30 to 50 candles. A triangle pattern usually takes much longer to form.

How to Trade Pennant pattern:
  • Identify the trend before the pattern develops.
  • Identify pattern which looks like a triangle structure moving Sideways and Horizontal
  • Draw a trend line on a series of lower highs followed by a series of lower lows. This froms a Support and Resistance
  • Wait for the Breakout of Support/Resistance
  • If the close price crosses the Support/Resistance line with a big candlesticks, This confirm the breakout
  • Increase in volume near the Support/Resistance line determines the perfect breakout
Target TAKE PROFIT:
  • The minimum target can be estimated using the technique of measuring the length/distance of the mast(initial Support and Resistance) and extending it in the direction of the breakout
Stop LOSS:
  • Stop level is just outside the pennant on the opposite side of the breakout.
