Flag patterns

A flag is a continuation pattern and is formed when the market consolidates in a narrow range after a sharp move. Usually a breakout from the flag is in the form of continuation of the prior trend.

The flag portion of the pattern must run between parallel lines and can either be slanted up, down, or even sideways.

Flag Pattern

There are two types of Flag Pattern. Depending on the direction of the movement and trend, Flag patterns are usually described as being bearish or bullish.


1. Bullish Flag:
Bullish Flag

Bullish Flag are continuation pattern that occurs as a brief pause in the up trend following a strong price move higher. The bull flag chart pattern looks like a downward sloping channel/rectangle denoted by two parallel trendlines against the preceding trend.



2. Bearish Flag:
Bearish Flag

Bearish Flag are continuation to an existing downward trend. The bear flag formation is underlined from an initial strong directional move down, followed by a consolidation channel in an upwards direction.



How to Trade Flag pattern:
Trading Flag pattern
  • Identify pattern which has downward/upward sloping price consolidation( Falg structure ) along with a long Pole structure in bullish/bearish movement as mentioned in the below image
  • Draw a trend line on a series of highs followed by a series of lows. This froms a Support and Resistance lines of the Flag
  • Wait for the Breakout of Support/Resistance
  • Bullish Flag: If the close price crosses the Resistance line with a big candlestick, This confirm the breakout and Up trend continues
  • Bearish Flag: If the close price crosses the Support line with a big candlestick, This confirm the breakout and Down trend continues
  • Increase in volume near the Support/Resistance breakout line determines the perfect breakout


Target TAKE PROFIT:
  • The minimum target can be estimated by measuring the hight/distance between Support and Resistance lines of the Flag and extending it in the direction of the breakout
  • Most of the time, the target will be more than the mentioned above step depending on how strong the trend is.
Entry and Exit
Stop LOSS:
  • Stop level should be placed at the mid point of the Support and Resistance line after the breakout
