Triangle pattern

A Triangle pattern is a consolidation pattern that occurs at mid-trend and usually signals a continuation of the existing trend. The triangle chart pattern is formed by drawing two converging trendlines as price temporarily moves in a sideways direction. On subsequent breakout, in the direction of the preceding trend, a trade can be done

Triangle pattern

A triangle pattern forms when a stockâ€™s trading range narrows following an uptrend or downtrend, usually indicating a consolidation, accumulation, or distribution before a continuation or reversal.


There are three types in Triangle pattern

1. Ascending Triangle:
Ascending Triangle

The ascending triangle is formed in an uptrend and indicates a continuation of the uptrend.

It is formed as a right-angled triangle with a resistance and a slope of higher lows.

The upper trendline(resistance line) is flat and the lower trendline(support line) is rising

The resistance does not allow the prices of the securities to move more upward. The higher lows show that the buying pressure has increased.

This pattern indicates that buyers are more aggressive than sellers as price continues to make higher lows.

This pattern clearly indicates that the market moves higher as the higher lows are formed heading toward the resistance line.

The breakout of the prices is confirmed when the prices break from the resistance level with volume and continue to move upwards.



2. Descending Triangle:
Descending Triangle

The descending triangle is formed in the downtrend and indicates the continuation of the downtrend.

It is formed as a downward sloping triangle with support and a slope of lower highs.

The lower trendline(support line) is flat and the upper trendline(resistance line) is falling.

The support does not allow the prices of the securities to move more downward.

The lower highs show that the selling pressure has increased.

This pattern indicates that sellers are more aggressive than buyers as price continues to make lower highs.

This pattern clearly indicates that the market moving lower as the lower highs are formed heading toward the support line.

This indicates the strength of bears and they are willing to sell more for the stock.

The breakout of the prices is confirmed when the prices break from the support level with volume and continue to move down.



3. Symmetrical Triangle:
Bullish Symmetrical Triangle
Bearish Symmetrical Triangle

A symmetrical triangle occurs when the price is making lower highs and higher lows.

Symmetrical triangle pattern is considered to be indecisive meaning that while the market is stuck within, the directional bias is unknown.

This means that neither the buyers nor the sellers are able to gain control, causing the price to range within a triangle.

Only the breakout of a boundary of a triangle clarifies the future price direction.

The price usually trades between trend lines which act as support and resistance. As the price breaks the support/resistance line of the triangle, trade can be taken based on the breakout direction.

Trading Psycology: Bulls (buyers) & bears (sellers) are fighting. As the fight intensifies, uncertainty increases and volume decreases, as the range of prices start narrowing. Once a breakout occurs, price and volume tends to spike, as all the buyers or sellers waiting on the sidelines start making their move.



How to trade Ascending/Descending Triangle:
  • For Ascending triangle: Identify the triangle structure having a flat Resistance and a rising Support Trendline moving Sideways
  • For Descending triangle: Identify the triangle structure having a flat Support and a rising Resistance Trendline moving Sideways
  • Draw a trend line on a series of Highs followed by a series of Lows. This froms a Support and Resistance line
  • Wait for the Breakout of Support/Resistance of the triangle respectively
  • If the close price crosses the Support/Resistance line with a big candlesticks, This confirm the breakout
  • Increase in volume near the Support/Resistance line determines the perfect breakout

How to trade Symmetrical Triangle:
  • Identify pattern which looks like a triangle structure moving Sideways and Horizontal
  • Draw a trend line on a series of lower Highs and higher Lows. This froms a Support and Resistance line
  • Wait for the close price break the Support or Resistance of the triangle
  • If the close price crosses the Support line with a big candlesticks, This confirms the breakout and Down Trend
  • If the close price crosses the Resistance line with a big candlesticks, This confirms the breakout and Up Trend
  • Increase in volume near the breakout determines the perfect breakout
Trading Symmetrical Triangle


Target TAKE PROFIT:
  • The minimum target can be estimated using the technique of measuring the length/distance between the initial Support and Resistance line and extending it in the direction of the breakout
Stop LOSS:
  • Stop level is just outside the triangle on the opposite side of the breakout.
