Cup and Handle pattern

A cup and handle pattern is a bullish continuation pattern. As its name implies, there are 2 parts to the pattern: the cup and the handle. The cup forms after an advance and looks like a bowl or rounding bottom.

As the cup like structure is completed in the chart, a trading range develops on the right hand side and forms like handle structure.

Once the pattern is complete and a subsequent breakout from the handleâ€™s trading range signals a continuation of trend in the prior advance

There are two types in Cup and handle pattern


1. Cup & Handle:
Cup & Handle

In these patterns, price gradually switches from bearish to bullish


2. Inverted Cup & Handle:
Inverted Cup & Handle

These are opposite to the Cup & Handle pattern. In these patterns, price gradually switches from bullish to bearish


Timeframe:
  • Applys to all timeframe
How to Trade:
  • Identify the cup and handle pattern in the chart
  • The cup should be U-shaped, not V-shaped.
  • The cup must have a handle on the right.
  • For Cup: Draw a horizontal line(Neck Line) across the top of the bearish and bullish sides of the round bottom or U pattern
  • For Handle: Draw support and resistance for the series of higher low an lower low for the symmetrical structure
  • As the close price crosses both the neck line and the resistance line of the Handle with large candle and increased volume
  • This confirms the breakout
Target TAKE PROFIT:
  • Measure the distance between the neck line and the lowest/highest point of the pattern and then apply the same distance to the downside/upside starting from the Neck Line respectively. This would be your minimum target profit.
Cup & Handle target with stop loss
Stop LOSS:
  • Stop level should be slightly above/below of the Handle's support/resistance line respectively
Falls Breakout:
Falls Breakout

After breakout, some times price can change its direction as mentioned above. To avoid huge Loss, please do always put a stop loss
