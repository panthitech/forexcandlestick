Shooting Star & Inverted Hammer

An Inverted Hammer and Shooting Star candlestick looks identical. The only difference between them is whether they appered in downtrend or uptrend.

Fig: Shooting Star & Inverted Hammer

Both candlesticks have little bodies, long upper shadows/wick, and small or no lower shadows/wick.

Inverted Hammer is a bullish reversal candlestick found in Down Trend, where as Shooting Star is a bearish reversal candlestick found in Up Trend.


1. Inverted Hammer

The Inverted Hammer forms when the price has been falling down and suggests the possibility of a reversal.

Its long upper shadow shows that buyers tried to bid the price higher but sellers still continues to push the price back down

As the battle ends, buyers made the price to close near the open

This indicates that everybody who wants to sell has already sold and no more sellers left. So now buyers count increases and price goes reversal to make Bullish trend.

Fig: HAMMER candle
  • Inverted Hammers must be seen at the bottom of a downtrend
  • Must wait for the next candle to confirm before entering on the trade
  • Inverted Hammer bottom fails if the next candles achieves a new low
  • Color of the body does not matter
  • Inverted Hammers do not provide any target, but it tells that traders are interested to go opposite to the down trend, means trend reversal might occur
  • Inverted Hammers always indicates the reversal signal and exit signal. Investors can utilize these signals to close out of their short positions.
  • Inverted Hammer's bottom or close can be considered as Support line
  • Please note- Inverted Hammers are not stronger that regular Hammers


2. Shooting Star

The Shooting Star is a bearish reversal pattern that looks identical to the inverted hammer but occurs when the price in uptrend.

Its long upper shadow shows that buyers attempted to push the price up, but more sellers came in to sell.

Since there are no more buyers left, price goes bearish.

Fig: Shooting Star
  • Shooting Star must be seen at the top of a uptrend
  • Must wait for the next candle to confirm before entering on the trade
  • This candlestick fails when a new high is achieved by the next candles
  • Color of the body does not matter
  • they do not provide any target, but it tells that traders are interested to go opposite to the up trend, means trend reversal might occur
  • Shooting Star always indicates the reversal signal and exit signal. Investors can utilize these signals to close out of their Long positions.
  • Shooting Star's Top or High can be considered as Resistance line
