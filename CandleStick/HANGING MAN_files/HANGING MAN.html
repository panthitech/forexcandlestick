HANGING MAN


Hanging Man Candlestick

The Hanging Man candlestick is found at the top of a uptrend and signals a potential bearish reversal trend in the market. This candle pattern also looks like a hammer.

Fig: HAMMER


It is similar to Hammer Candlestick, but Hammer candlestick forms in Downtrend whereas Hanging man forms in Up trend

Aggressive buy from the open of the candle and aggressive sell, make market to go down more from the top and price closes near to the top. This forms the Hammer candle.

  • Hanging man candles are seen at the top of a uptrend
  • Shadow are ideally 2-3 times the size of the body
  • Color of the body does not matter, i.e it has both bullish and bearish bodies
  • Hanging man is not considered as bearish as the Shooting Star
  • Must wait for the next candle to confirm before entering to the trade
  • A Hanging man fails when a new high is achieved by next candles
  • They do not provide any target, but it tells that traders are interested to go opposite to the up trend, means trend reversal might occur
  • Hanging man always indicates the reversal signal and exit signal. Investors can utilize these signals to close out of their Long positions.
  • Hanging man's Top or Close can be considered as Resistance line
