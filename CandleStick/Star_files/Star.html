Star


Morning/Evening Star candles

Candles with small body and having a gap up/down in the chart, forms a strong reversal in trend called as Star candles. These have three candles in the pattern, which can form on the chart after a particular trend. These are the trend reversal patterns.

The middle candle should open with a gap up/down based on the trend

Fig: Morning Star and Evening Star

There are 2 variants in this candlestick pattern.

1. Morning Star Candlestick Pattern

The ‘Morning Star’ is a three candle pattern, which occur on the chart after a down trend. It is a bullish trend reversal pattern.

In a downtrend, in any candle, there will be huge sell off and the price declines creating a red candle on the chart. This form first Red candle.

On the next candle, the selling continues with a gap down opening. When the prices get low, the buyers step in and start buying. This forms second candle.

On the next candle, the bears(sellers) begin to lose their conviction as buying continues. The bears begin to cover their shorts, the stock rises further. This creates a third Green candle

Morning star indicates that sellers have failed, and buyers are dominating the market.

Please note that, this same 3 candle in a higher time frame looks like Hammer.

Fig: Morning Star Candle
  • It is bullish trend reversal Pattern
  • The first candle is red this is bearish.
  • The second candle can be any colour, but it should be a 'Doji' or 'Spinning Top' which suggests indecision
  • The third candle is green or bullish with a large volume.
  • Position can be taken when the fourth candle opens in green.
  • The bottom of the second candle is the Stop Loss.
  • After bullish action, most of the time, market retraces back to same level for the price correction
  • Must wait for the fourth candle to confirm before putting on the trade

NOTE: Similar to Morning Star candlestick patterns- Morning Doji Star and Bullish Abandoned Baby candlestick pattern follow the same rules as mentioned above. Only changes is with the second middle candle's body and size as mentioned below.

2. Evening Star Candlestick Pattern

Evening star is the opposite of the Morning star pattern.

The ‘Evening Star’ is a three candle pattern, which occur on the chart after a up trend. It is a bearish trend reversal pattern.

Evening star indicates that buyers have failed, sellers are dominating the market.

Fig: Evening Star Candles
  • It is bearish trend reversal Pattern
  • The first candle is Green and bullish.
  • The second candle can be any colour, but it should be a 'Doji' or 'Spinning Top' which suggests indecision
  • The third candle is red or bearish with a large volume.
  • Position can be taken when the fourth candle opens in red.
  • The top of the second candle is the Stop Loss.
  • After bearish action, most of the time, market retraces back to same level for the price correction
  • Must wait for the fourth candle to confirm before putting on the trade

NOTE: Similar to Evening Star candlestick patterns- Evening Doji Star and Bearish Abandoned Baby candlestick pattern follow the same rules as mentioned above. Only changes is with the second middle candle's body and size as mentioned below.
