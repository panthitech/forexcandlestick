HARAMI


Harami Candles

The Harami candlestick pattern is a trading strategy used to identify potential trend reversals or continuation in the price action

Fig: Harami candles

Harami candlestick pattern is a 2-candle pattern, which can form on the chart after a short term Trend. The second candle will have a Gaps at higher or lower. This gap makes different while compare to other candlesticks.

Note: Candles opening with Gap up or down can be found in Stock markets, but not on Crypto currency, Commodity and Forex markets. Because they are 24/7 markets. For these markets, gaps can be ignored.


Fig: Harami Definition

The name, “Harami”, comes from the Japanese word ‘pregnant’. The formation of the 2 candles looks like pregnant woman. Where large candle looks like women carries the baby small candle inside her.


Harami candlestick has 2 variants as below.

1. Bullish Harami pattern

A bullish Harami candle signals that a downtrend is about to end and a bullish reversal can be expected to start.

In a down trend, the sellers are in control of the market, in sudden when a big red candle with a new low is formed, reinforcing the sellers position in the market. This forms the first candle.

On next candle, the market opens at a price higher than the previous candle's close. This panics sellers as they expected lower opening price. Later market closes below previous candle high. This forms second candle.

Since market gets a strength from Buyers, we can expect the next candle to be the Bullish candle.

Fig: Bullish Harami Candle
  • The bullish Harami candle formed at the bottom of the downtrend and it signals uptrend reversal
  • The pattern involves two candles, the first candle has to be large and red and The second candle should be green
  • After bullish action, most of the time, market retraces back to same level for the price correction
  • Must wait for the next candle to confirm before putting on the trade


2. Bearish Harami pattern

The Bearish Harami pattern is the opposite of the Bullish Harami pattern.

A bearish Harami candle signals end of uptrend and we can expect bearish reversal.

In uptrend, a huge green candle occurs due to buyers in control. This forms the first candle.

On next candle, the market opens at a price lower than the previous candle's close. This panics buyers as they expected higher opening price. Later market closes above previous candle open. This forms second candle.

As buyers pauses adding stocks, sellers will have higher strength. By this we can expect the next candle to be the Bearish candle.

Fig: Bearish Harami Candles
  • The bearish Harami candle formed at the top of the uptrend and it signals bearish reversal
  • The pattern involves two candles, the first candle has to be large Green candle and The second candle should be red
  • After bearish action, most of the time, market retraces back to same level for the price correction
  • Must wait for the next candle to confirm before putting on the trade
