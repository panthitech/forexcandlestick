Japanese Candlestick

A candlestick chart represents the price movement of a stock market over a certain time period. These are formed by OHLC (Open, High, Low and Close) price of the market.


Fig1: Japanese Candlestick

Japanese Candlesticks are broken down into two main parts, the body and the wick( also called as shadow). These parts meet in a style that looks like a candle, thus the name of the chart style as Candlestick.

Wicks or Shadows

Fig1: Wicks or Shadows
Thin line at the top and bottom of the candle body, shows the highest and lowest prices traded over the time frame forms the Wicks

A candle with a short or no wick means that price action was strong in to the close and either buyers, represented with a green candlestick, or sellers, represented with a red candlestick, were in control for the entire time period.

If the wicks are long, then there was a large range of prices traded and buyers and sellers were both in control.


Fig1: Candlestick Body and Wick by size
Body
The thicker middle portion, shows the open and closing prices during the time frame forms the Body of the candlestick

Fig1: Candlestick body

The size of the body indicates the intensity of buying or selling. The longer the body, the more price moved over the time period of that candlestick.

A short body means that the opening and closing prices were very similar, meaning that there was not much strength to price action.


Note: It is not mandatory to be the color of Bullish candle as Green and Bearish candle as Red. You can change it to your personal choice.


Based on Open and Close price, candles can be distinguished in 2 types

1. Bullish Candle

These Bullish candles are formed when the market price Closes above the Open price over a particular time period.

Fig1: Bullish Candle
  • Close price higher than Open price
  • Usually White or Green color are used to represent bullish candle
  • May or may not have wicks
  • Tells market in up trend

2. Bearish Candle

These Bearish candles are formed when the market price Closes below the Open price over a particular time period.

Fig1: Bearish Candle
  • Close price lower than Open price
  • Usually Black or Red color are used to represent bearish candle
  • May or may not have wicks
  • Tells market in down trend
