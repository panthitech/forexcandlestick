Hammer Candlestick

The Hammer candlestick is found at the bottom of a downtrend and signals a potential bullish reversal trend in the market. The candle pattern looks like a hammer and it's body can be any color, i.e Red or Green.

Fig: HAMMER candle

Aggressive huge sell from the open of the candle and recovers back again and price closes near to the high. This forms the Hammer candle. This indicates that sellers lost their interest in the market, so Buyers comes in.

Below image shows how Hammer looks in shorter timeframe. Three 8 hour candles forms a 1 day Hammer candle.

Fig: HAMMER formation


  • Hammers must be seen at the bottom of a downtrend
  • Shadow are ideally 2-3 times the size of the body
  • Color of the body does not matter
  • Must wait for the next candle to confirm before entering on the trade
  • A Hammer fails when a new high is achieved immediately after completion, and a hammer bottom fails if the next candle achieves a new low
  • Hammers do not provide any target, but it tells that traders are interested to go opposite to the down trend, means trend reversal might occur
  • Hammers always indicates the reversal signal and exit signal. Investors can utilize these signals to close out of their short positions.
  • Hammer's bottom or close can be considered as Support line
