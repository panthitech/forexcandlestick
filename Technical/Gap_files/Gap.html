Gap


Gap on Charts

Gaps represent an area on the chart where the price of the stock moves swiftly either up or down. Gaps on the charts occurs for many reason.

Gaps

Gaps typically occur when a piece of news or an event announced by the stock companies. This makes buyers or sellers curious about that stock. If the News is positive, buyers buy the stocks higher than usual price. If the News is negative, all the sellers try to sell their holdings at cheaper price.

Gaps can often form in a market due to strong price action. These gaps can be an indication of a momentum or a change in direction.

Gaps also seen after Harami or Doji patterns

Most of the times, when a gap forms, markets may retrace to close that gap.

Note: Candles opening with Gap up or down can be found in Stock markets, but rarely on Crypto currency, Commodity and Forex markets. Because they are 24/7 markets.

Gap types
  • Gaps can only be observed on the candlestick chart or the bar chart
  • Gaps provides information about the price movement
  • Genuine or valid gaps occur when the market skips a price level
  • If the gap direction is upside, then it is called as GapUP and its Bullish sign
  • If the gap direction is downside, then it is called as GapDown and its a Bearish sign
  • Most of the time, gaps acts as trend reversal pattern
  • Sometimes in a strong trend after pattern breakout, we can see the Gaps. This type of gapes are called as breakaway gaps.
  • Most of the time, after the gap formation, market tries to fill that gap
