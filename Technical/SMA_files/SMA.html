Moving Average

Moving averages are the average prices of the past 'x' number of periods. These can help to form the Support and Resistance for the price action

Moving average are frequently used technical indicator. It reduces the price fluctuations or noise. It filters small fluctuations in the price so the viewer can see the price movement without much fluctuations and noise on the chart.

Moving Averages are the lagging indicators, which means they do not predict where price is going, they are only providing data on where price has been.

There are multiple types of moving averages. Most popular and commonly used moving averages are as follows

  • SMA - Simple Moving Average
  • EMA - Exponential Moving Average
Fig-1: Moving average example
SMA - Simple Moving Average:
A simple moving average (SMA) are the continues average of a selected range of prices, usually closing prices, by the number of periods in that range.

EMA - Exponential Moving Average:
EMA are similar to SMA but these are exponentially weighted moving averages. They places a greater weight and significance on the price of the most recent data points. It reacts quicker to the changes in the price movements than the SMA.

Note: Few markets works good with SMA but not on EMA, and again few works properly with EMA and not with SMA.

Moving average periods:

Based on traders comfortability, the length and period can be set to any of the Moving average(i.e SMA, EMA or other) indicator. For slow moving average a longer periods are preferred and for fast moving average a shorter periods are preferred as follows

7 to 10 period: It is a very popular and extremely fast moving average. Often used as a directional filter. Very useful for short term trade, Future and Option trading.

20 to 25 period: It is a Medium-term and the most accurate moving average. Most of the time this shows the exact price movement. Good when it comes to riding trends.

50 period: It is a Medium-term moving average and in a volatile and trend market, it is best suited for identifying the mid-term reversal and direction.

100 period: It is Long-term moving average. Can be used for the direction of the market.

200 period: This is commonly used long-term moving average which acts as Support and Resistance for most of the stocks. This is also used as trend indicator, in which the current price above 200 period moving average line then can be considered as market is Bullish and price goes below the line as Bearish


Note: It is not mandatory to use multiple periods, cross overs to enter the trade. It's up-to you that how much you are comfortable at using a Moving average to take a decision. A good trader always wait for the opportunity to enter into the trade at right time.



How to use Moving Averages:

There are multiple ways to use the moving average. Below are the few examples. Please note that all the bellow examples applies to most of the different types of Moving averages(i.e SAM, EMA and others)


1. Cross-over strategy

This is the easiest strategy. If fast moving MA crosses above slow moving MA, then market can be considered as Bullish. In the same way, fast moving MA crosses below slow moving MA, then it can be considered as Bearish. For this, need at least 2 Moving periods.

MA Cross over

Another method is, If last price crosses above any MA line, then market can be considered as Bullish. If last price crosses below any MA line, then market can be considered as Bearish.



2. The Golden Cross and the Death Cross Strategies

The Golden Cross is a bullish signal when the 50 period moving average crosses above the 200 period moving average. When the market is in a long-term downtrend, the 50-day moving average is below the 200-day moving average. When a new uptrend begins, the 50 period moving average crosses above the 200 moving average. This cross over is called as Golden Cross Strategy.

The Death Cross is a bearish signal when the 50 period moving average crosses below the 200 period moving average. It is exactly opposite to the Golden cross strategy

Golden Cross and the Death Cross example
  • It works properly in trend and volatile market
  • It is a Lag indicator, you might miss out the exact entry of the trade
  • Usually preferred for long term trading
  • Gives falls signals in Side ways market
  • Use multiple indicator to enter the trade
  • Stop loss can be set by Trend line or Lowest price of the previous candles


3. Dynamic Support and Resistance Strategies

As we know that Support and Resistance can be set as Horizontal lines, Diagonal lines and by Trend lines. In the same way, Moving Averages also acts as Dynamic support and resistance.

Dynamic support and resistance levels are constantly changing and moving with the price. When price dips and tests the moving average or touches the moving average line, trader can take a action of Buy or Sell, but not every time it touches. Fig.3 is an example for that.

Trade can taken based on price support and resistance, or according to its breakout of that line

Dynamic Support and Resistance example
  • It works well with Elliott wave pattern theory(Advanced Trading)
  • Most of the traders use this for Scalping (Advanced trading)
  • Since 90% people use moving average with period 10, 20, 50, 100 and 200 while trading, all gonna expect the price movement according to the moving average line. So we can consider its level as a dynamic Support and Resistance.
  • Dynamic support and resistance are not as strong as Horizontal and Diagonal support and resistance
  • Can be used as level check
  • Encounters with falls breakout
  • Applicable to multiple time frame

Timeframe:
  • Applies to all timeframe
  • Moving Averages work better if applied on multiple timeframe for the same market

Pros and Cons of using Moving Averages

PROS

  • Acts as Support and Resistance
  • Used to determine the trend
  • Most of the traders use Moving average, so we can take advantage of that

CONS

  • Its a lag indicator. Signals or cross over happens after price movements. Opportunities can be missed out
  • Better to use trend lines or another indicators
