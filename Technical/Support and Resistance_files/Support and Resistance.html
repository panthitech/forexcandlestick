Support and Resistance Levels

One can become a pro trader if they have highly knowledge on RSI, candlestick patterns, Support and Resistance levels with Price Action.

All the technical indicators that we have today are derivatives of price and cannot give you more meaningful information than price alone.

In order to read the price information correctly, we should understand the concept of support and resistance.

Support and Resistance

We can find some levels from where the direction of the market starts to reverse its trend, those levels are known as Support and Resistance levels.

Support is a price area on a chart, where demand is located

Resistance is a price area, where supply is located

Supply and Demand

In simple terms, Demand is a zone in which Buyers are more interested to buy the stocks and Supply is a zone in which Sellers are waiting to liquidate their holdings.

These levels forms a trend line called Support and Resistance. Using support and resistance, we can predict the price movement and direction any market



How to identify Support and Resistance levels?

Below are few different methods to identify support or resistance levels

  1. Find trend reversal points of price in a chart of any stock and try to draw combined trend line over those points. This forms support and resistance



  2. Find demand and supply zone to identify support and resistance level.



  3. Using Higher high and lower lows as support & resistance



  4. Using multiple time frames to identify support and resistance. This is known as Pivot level.



  5. Using Moving average as support and resistance







Things to remember about Support and Resistance levels

Resistance to support
  • Resistance will become Support, if price crosses that level and sustains above it
  • Support will become Resistance, if price cross it and sustains below it
  • Support and Resistance zones are very strong because Market psychology plays a major role as traders and investors remember the past and react to changing conditions to anticipate future market movement.
  • Support and Resistance line are not always parallel each other. They can be slope and diverged too.
  • Most of the traders are more sentimental and emotional towards support and resistance zones


How to trade:
  • Identify trend reversal points and draw trendline forming support and resistance
  • Buy at support level and Sell at Resistance level
  • If support level breaks and price sustains below, short the stock
  • If resistance level breaks and price sustains above, buy the stock
  • Do not forget to put the Stoploss


Timeframe:
  • Applies to all time-frame
  • Support and Resistance lines work better if applied on multiple time-frame for the same market

Pros and Cons

PROS

  • The market entry and exit points can be determined
  • Shows the strength of the market trend and momentum of the market
  • Can determine the reversal of the trend
  • Can be used in any time frame
  • Most of the traders use Support & Resistance for trading. So we can expect with higher probability to make decision over market.
  • Suitable for sideways market
  • Can be applied on all types of trading charts

CONS

  • Trend points will not be in same level or zones, so support and resistance lines should be adjusted to reach those points
  • Fake-outs can occur most of the time
