MACD


MACD-Moving Average Convergence Divergence

MACD stands for Moving Average Convergence Divergence and it is a price Momentum indicator.

MACD indicator gives the short to medium term trend of the price action. A positive MACD value indicates upward price trend while a negative MACD value indicates a downward price trend.

  • If MACD line crosses ABOVE the zero line, this signals an UPTREND
  • If MACD line crosses BELOW the zero line, this signals an DOWNTREND
MACD example

MACD composites of

The MACD line: It is the difference between two exponential moving averages (EMA) of different periods: commonly 12 and 26 periods

The Signal line: It is the 9-period moving average of the MACD line

The Central line: It is the Zero refernce line of MACD

The Histogram: It is the difference between the MACD line and the signal line


MACD Crossover Strategy:

When the price is in a trend and the MACD makes a crossover with signal line, it indicates that the Buy or Sell signal

1. Bullish Crossover:
MACD Bullish Crossover

When the MACD line is below Zero level and crosses Signal line, this indicating that the market may soon be turning bullish.

Few common points to be obeserved before taking trade

  • MACD Histograms making Higher lows(i.e indicating uptrend)
  • When the MACD line crosses above the signal line, then this indicates BUY signal
  • Target can be set till MACD line crosses below signal line
  • Stop loss can be set by Trend line or Lowest price of the previous candles

2. Bearish Crossover:
MACD Bearish Crossover

When the MACD line is above Zero level and crosses below the Signal line, this indicating that the market may soon be turning Bearish.

Few common points to be obeserved before taking trade

  • When the MACD line crosses below the signal line, then this indicates SELL signal
  • Target can be set till MACD line crosses above signal line
  • Stop loss can be set by Trend line or highest price of the previous candles


MACD Divergence Strategy:

Trade can be done based on the divergence.

1. Bullish Divergence:
MACD Bullish Divergence

When the price is on a downtrend but the MACD is on an uptrend, it signals a bullish divergence, indicating that the market may soon be turning bullish.

Few common points to be obeserved before taking trade

  • The price making lower lows (i.e downtrend)
  • MACD Histograms making Higher lows(i.e indicating uptrend)
  • When the MACD line crosses above the signal line, then this indicates BUY signal
  • Target can be set till MACD line crosses below signal line
  • Stop loss can be set by Trend line or Lowest price of the previous candles
2. Bearish Divergence:
MACD Bearish Divergence

When the price is on an uptrend but the MACD is on a downtrend, it signals a bearish divergence, indicating that the market may soon be turning bearish.

Few common points to be obeserved before taking trade

  • The price making higher highs (i.e uptrend)
  • MACD Histograms making lower highs(i.e indicating downtrend)
  • When the MACD line crosses below the signal line, then this indicates SELL signal
  • Target can be set till MACD line crosses above signal line
  • Stop loss can be set by Trend line or Highest price of the previous candles

Pros and Cons of using MACD

PROS

  • Shows strength of the market trend and momentum of the market
  • Shows over brought and over sold areas
  • Can determine the reversal of the trend
  • Works good in volatile and high momentum market
  • Work best in trend market

CONS

  • Lots of false signals
  • Its a lag indicator. Signal can obtain after price action. Opportunities can be missed
  • Need another indicator for the proper entry of the signal
  • Does not suitable for sideways market
